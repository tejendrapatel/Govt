About

The Project



The Mohanpura and Kundalia Irrigation Projects are the first and biggest SCADA-enabled Automated Pressurized Irrigation Network (PIN) projects in India that supply water to agricultural fields. They now encompass 96,000 hectares over seven sub-project regions. We are located in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh's Rajgarh District and started in 2018. Out of the 14.50 lakh residents of Mohanpura and Kundalia, 3.50 lakh are farmers who benefit from the irrigation project.
This project would provide irrigation water for agriculture on 58,000 hectares of land in Mohanpura and 20,000 hectares of land in Kundalia. Mohanpura's total number of benefitted villages is 776, whereas Kundalia's total number of helped villages is 419.

Awaiting a Life-Changing Opportunity

Farmers in the Kundalia region are hopeful that the project would bring water to their farms. Getting water in the fields will be a life-changing opportunity for farmers here, says Pop Singh Sisodiya of Lasuldiya village. Dilip Singh of Pilwas village says, “Farmers are getting high yields in the areas where they have received water. I’m hopeful that such transformation will take place in my village too.”

Celebrating Holi in Their Own Lands

Unlike previous years when they would be in some foreign land, on construction sites or fields, the joyous farmers celebrated Holi in their village. The village now cultivates its fields and makes enough money to live off it all year long. Rajgarh's district collector, Mr.Harsh Dixit on the day of the Hindu festival of colors Holi, Harsh Dixit, and Mr.Vikas Rajoria, Project Administrator of the Mohanpura and Kundalia irrigation projects, were welcomed with warmth and color at Machalpur village. The neighborhood welcomed them with fresh, green wheat ears, decorations, music, dancing, and festivities. Why would we toil in other places when we have water for our farms, asks farmer Bhansilal Tawar? Working on our grounds and producing brings us great delight say the localities’.

Minister Of Civil Aviation

On April 17, 2022, Jyotiraditya Scindia, the Indian government's minister of civil aviation, paid a visit to the Mohanpura pump house. The project's socio-cultural effects were briefly discussed by Vikas Rajoria, the project's administrator. The idea was praised by the minister of civil aviation, who noted that it is not just increasing hectare-level yield while simultaneously reducing migration He praised the police officers and employees of the project for the project's effective execution. He said “Such kinds of projects will contribute to the development of the region. The success of this project will serve as an inspiration for the other projects in the country.”
